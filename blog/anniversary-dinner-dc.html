Anniversary Dinner in DC: Where to Book From Year 1 to Year 20

Anniversary dinner planning is a specific category of stress because the stakes feel higher than they are. You want it to feel celebratory but not forced. Memorable but not ostentatious. Expensive enough to signal that you care, but not so expensive that it becomes "the year we ate $400 worth of dinner we can't really describe anymore." Here's the calibration, by milestone.

First anniversary: Rose's Luxury

You're in the casually serious phase. The relationship is real but you're still having fun with it. Rose's Luxury is perfect for this moment because the vibe is warm and playful, the food is creative without being fussy, and the room has that "I love this place" energy that translates into the dinner itself. Book the 7:30. Ask for a table by the window if you can. Review →

Second anniversary: Le Diplomate

The classic. You're now a real couple and you're celebrating with a classic DC date. Book a banquette. Order the steak frites. Share the profiteroles. This is a no-risk, high-reward anniversary move that will feel great every single time. Details →

Third anniversary: Albi

By year three you're ready for a tasting menu experience and you want something that feels transportive. Albi's open-fire Levantine cooking is one of the best full-experience meals in DC. The room is intimate, the bread comes out hot, the wine pairings are intelligent. Why Albi →

Fourth anniversary: The Dabney

Wood-fire cooking in Blagden Alley. Hidden, special, seasonal. Book the chef's counter for a fully immersive experience — you can watch the live fire cooks work and chat with them between courses. This is the year you start curating the experience, not just the restaurant.

Fifth anniversary: Pineapple and Pearls

Major milestone. The full two-Michelin-star tasting menu. Book three months out — not kidding. The pacing is perfect for an anniversary: long enough to feel like an event, short enough that you're not catatonic by the time you leave. This is a five-hour experience that will define the year.

Sixth through ninth: rotating the classics

Years six through nine are the "keep it fresh" years. Rotate through the classics: Bresca, Fiola Mare, Blue Duck Tavern, Little Pearl, Imperfecto. Each has a distinct personality. Treat the rotation as part of the ritual — "which classic are we doing this year?"

Tenth anniversary: Fiola Mare

Ten years deserves the river view and the full Fabio Trabocchi treatment. The table-for-two by the window with the Potomac behind you is genuinely one of the most romantic settings in DC. Order the branzino. Get a bottle of something Italian and expensive. This is not a night to pick from the by-the-glass list. Review →

Fifteenth anniversary: Minibar by José Andrés

Theater of food. Eight to twelve guests at a time, chef-driven multi-course tasting, theatrical presentation. This is a "once in a decade" restaurant and the 15th anniversary is the right moment. Book six months out. This will be one of the five most memorable meals of your marriage, period.

Twentieth anniversary: Kinship

Quietly perfect. Eric Ziebold's Michelin-starred dining room organized around the five sections (Craft, History, Ingredients, Indulgence, For the Table). Twenty years earns a restaurant that feels like a quiet victory lap, not a flashy celebration. Kinship delivers exactly that.

The strategies that actually matter

Call, don't just book online. When you book, call the restaurant and say it's an anniversary. Name the year. They'll note it and you'll get a better table, a small amenity (a dessert, a card, a glass of champagne), and more attentive service. This is free and works every single time. Restaurants love anniversaries.

Book earlier than you think. For Michelin-level spots, three months is standard. Rose's Luxury and Pineapple and Pearls can require six months. Bresca, Fiola Mare, and Blue Duck Tavern need at least a month.

Give the same gift (or don't, but choose). Some couples have the tradition of "we always go to a new restaurant" on anniversaries. Others have "we go back to the place we had our first date." Both are beautiful. The key is choosing one intentionally so it becomes a ritual, not a scramble.

The first course at home. My favorite anniversary hack: have a glass of champagne at home before you leave. It slows the night down, creates a ritual moment that's just between the two of you, and means you arrive at the restaurant already feeling celebratory instead of rushing in from traffic.
