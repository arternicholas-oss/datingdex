The Best DC Rooftop Bars for Dates (That Aren't Just "Tall and Windy")

Rooftop bars are the dating equivalent of a peacock opening its tail. Yes, everybody can see it. Yes, we're doing it anyway. The question isn't whether to go rooftop — it's which one won't disappoint.

Top of the Gate (Watergate Hotel)

The Potomac view is unserious-level good. The cocktails are solid. Your date will take a photo. It's fine. Let it happen.

Wild Days at the Eaton Hotel

Lush, plant-filled, great for golden-hour photos and pretending you live in Brooklyn.

12 Stories at the Intercontinental Wharf

Views of the Potomac and the monuments. This is the "I mean business" rooftop.

Wunder Garten (NoMa)

Not technically a rooftop but open-air, beer-garden-coded, and way less pretentious. Details →

A warning about rooftops

If it's windy and your date has long hair, they will spend 40% of the conversation wrangling it. Check the forecast. Adjust accordingly.
