Cheap Date Ideas in DC That Don't Feel Cheap (Under $30 Total)

Here's the thing about cheap dates: the problem is never that they're cheap. The problem is when they feel cheap. There's a difference between "we had $14 beers on a rooftop" and "I took you to CVS and bought us one granola bar." Both are technically affordable. Only one will get a second date.

1. Tidal Basin walk + Call Your Mother bagels (Under $15)

Grab two bagels (objectively the best in DC), walk the Tidal Basin, and pretend to know things about cherry blossoms. Works year-round. The bagels do 90% of the flirting for you.

2. National Gallery of Art + People-Watching ($0)

It's free. It's enormous. There's a fountain room in the basement that looks like a Wes Anderson movie. Making up fake backstories for strangers in the galleries is a legitimate bonding activity, and if art bores them, you've learned something important.

3. Filter Coffeehouse + a walk through Dupont ($10)

Two coffees, one loop around the neighborhood, a bookstore stop at Kramers. Filter is the move →

4. Yards Park + Bluejacket half-flight ($18)

Walk the park, split a beer flight at Bluejacket, stare at boats. The boats will do some of the work.

5. Eastern Market on a Saturday ($20 if you're snacking)

Daytime first dates in a market setting are underrated. You're walking, pointing at things, eating samples, laughing at weird pottery. Low pressure, high chemistry detection.

6. Trivia night at Wonderland Ballroom ($15 for drinks)

Nothing exposes compatibility faster than watching someone argue about whether "Alanis Morissette" counts as the 90s.

7. Ben's Chili Bowl pilgrimage ($12)

A half-smoke and a shared fries, and now you've had a Date With History™. Tell them Obama ate there. They'll pretend to care.

8. Kenilworth Aquatic Gardens ($0)

The most underrated free date in DC. Lotus flowers in July, herons year-round, 0% chance of running into your ex.

9. Sticky Fingers + a Columbia Heights wander ($18)

Vegan bakery, then walk. If they say "I didn't know vegan baking could be good," you've already won the date.
