How to Pick a Date Restaurant Without Sending 47 Texts

Here's the framework. Save it. Print it. Tattoo it.

Step 1: What's the stage?

First date → low pressure + easy exit. Second → food-forward. Third+ → activity or scene. Anniversary → Albi. You're welcome.

Step 2: What's the budget?

Under $40 pp → All-Purpose, Bar Charley, Doi Moi happy hour.
$40–$80 → Le Diplomate, Maydan, Unconventional Diner.
$80+ → Bresca, Fiola Mare, The Dabney, Albi.

Step 3: What's the exit plan?

A good first date has an easy out. Pick a place where "I should get going" doesn't require an exit interview. Small plates and bars beat four-course tasting menus every time.

Step 4: Have you asked them if they have dietary restrictions?

Ask. Before booking. This is not a loyalty test. This is basic.

Step 5: Book it and stop deliberating

The worst thing you can do is send five "what do you think of this" texts. The best thing you can do is say "booked us at X at 7:30, let me know if that doesn't work." Decisiveness is attractive. Deliberation is not.
