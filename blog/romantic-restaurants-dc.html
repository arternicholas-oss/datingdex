The 9 Most Romantic Restaurants in DC (Not Just "Dim Lighting")

"Romantic" is the single most abused word in restaurant marketing. Every place with a tealight candle calls itself romantic. Meanwhile you're eating a $38 linguine under fluorescent lighting next to a bachelorette party. Here are the places that actually deliver.

1. Fiola Mare (Georgetown)

River views, hushed dining room, seafood so good it could save a struggling relationship. This is the "I'm serious about you" restaurant. Full review →

2. Rose's Luxury (Capitol Hill)

Warm, inventive, and just loud enough that you can whisper without being heard. Small plates mean more things to share, and sharing food is the original love language. See why it's #1 →

3. Pineapple and Pearls (Capitol Hill)

Tasting menu, impeccable service, the kind of place where the bill is high but nobody cares because you're in love. Details →

4. Albi (Navy Yard)

Michelin-starred Middle Eastern. The lighting is correct. The bread is correct. The first kiss outside is practically scheduled. Review →

5. The Dabney (Shaw)

Wood fire, intimate dining room, the kind of seasonal menu that gives you things to talk about beyond "so how's work."

6. Bresca (Logan Circle)

Michelin-starred, small, and the kind of date restaurant that makes you re-evaluate every other date restaurant.

7. Kinship (Mount Vernon Triangle)

Chef Eric Ziebold's quietly gorgeous fine dining room. The menu is organized by "craft, history, ingredients, indulgence, for the table." Romance is baked in.

8. Little Pearl (Capitol Hill)

Tiny wine bar vibes with serious food. The scale forces intimacy.

9. Imperfecto (West End)

Mediterranean-Latin fusion with a dining room that feels like a secret. Ideal for the "I've been holding this in for months" dinner.
